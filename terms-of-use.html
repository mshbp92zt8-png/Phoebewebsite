Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 2026  ·  Effective date: July 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how your personal information is collected, used, stored, and shared when you receive occupational therapy coaching or attend experiential group workshops (online or in person) from BodyMindOT, and when you use this website. It sets out your rights under UK data protection law and is written in accordance with the UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018. It should be read alongside the BodyMindOT Practice Policy and Cookie Policy.

1. Who We Are & Practice Details

BodyMindOT is an independent occupational therapy practice, the trading name of Phoebe Hsieh (Wei-Yun Hsieh), an HCPC-registered Occupational Therapist. Services are provided in accordance with Health and Care Professions Council (HCPC) standards, the Royal College of Occupational Therapists (RCOT) Code of Ethics, and UK data protection law.

For the purposes of data protection law, BodyMindOT is the Data Controller of your personal information — responsible for deciding how and why your data is processed, stored, and protected.

Practice name: BodyMindOT
Lead Therapist & Data Controller: Phoebe Hsieh (Wei-Yun Hsieh) — HCPC Registration OT80369
Email: phoebehsieh@bodymindot.com

Target client group: Our services are provided strictly to adults aged 18 or over. We do not knowingly process the personal data of anyone under the age of 18.

Services offered: (1) one-to-one online occupational therapy coaching supporting everyday mental wellbeing and daily functioning; and (2) experiential group workshops (online or in person) exploring creativity, movement, and wellbeing in everyday life.

2. Information We Collect

To provide safe, effective, and professional care, and to run this website, we collect and process the following categories of personal data:

  • Contact & enquiry data: your name, email address, and anything you include in our website contact form.
  • Identity & contact details: full name, date of birth, home address, phone number, and email address.
  • Emergency contact details: the name, relationship, and telephone number of your designated emergency contact.
  • GP & referrer details: your GP's contact details (where you consent to provide them) or any third-party professional who referred you.
  • Health, therapy & workshop data: clinical coaching notes, assessments, medical histories, details of mental/physical health conditions, and therapy goals (special category data under UK GDPR). For workshops, attendance records, safety confirmations, and workshop-specific consent.
  • Administrative & payment records: appointment schedules, billing details, payment transaction histories, communication records (including emails and secure messages), and virtual connection logs.
  • Technical data (via the website): IP address (collected automatically by our hosting provider), browser type and version, and pages visited/time spent (if analytics are active).

We do not ask you to submit health information through the website — any such information is provided entirely at your discretion.

3. How We Collect Your Data

  • Directly from you: through your enquiry, the registration and consent forms, the initial assessment, and during your sessions or workshops.
  • Contact form or email: when you submit an enquiry via the website or contact us directly by email.
  • Automatically: technical data collected by our web hosting provider when you visit the site.

4. Lawful Basis for Processing

Under UK data protection law, we rely on the following lawful bases:

  • Contract (Article 6(1)(b)): to deliver the coaching or workshops you have requested, manage bookings, and complete administration.
  • Legal obligation (Article 6(1)(c)): to comply with legal, regulatory, and professional responsibilities, such as keeping accurate healthcare records in line with HCPC and RCOT standards.
  • Legitimate interests (Article 6(1)(f)): to respond to enquiries you have sent to us.
  • Consent (Article 6(1)(a)): for any optional communications, where explicitly obtained.
  • Vital interests (Article 6(1)(d)): in extreme circumstances, to protect your life or safety (e.g. an acute medical emergency during an online session or in-person workshop).

For special category (health) data, we rely on Article 9(2)(h) of the UK GDPR (provision of health and social care, managed by a registered healthcare professional bound by professional secrecy), and on explicit consent (Article 9(2)(a)) where applicable.

5. How We Use Your Data

  • To respond to your enquiry or consultation request;
  • To provide and manage occupational therapy coaching and workshops;
  • To maintain professional clinical records as required by HCPC standards;
  • To manage bookings, payments, and administration; and
  • To improve the website and user experience (using aggregated, anonymised analytics).

We will never sell your personal data, or share it with third parties for their own marketing purposes.

6. Storage, Security & Systems

All client records and communications are stored securely using Google Workspace Business. Access is strictly controlled, password-protected, and secured with two-step verification (2FA) and encryption in transit and at rest. Google acts as our data processor under Google's Cloud Data Processing Addendum.

For clients temporarily joining sessions from outside the UK, the service remains governed by the laws and exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of England and Wales. During temporary travel overseas, we continue to apply the same security standards — sessions conducted from private, confidential spaces, on secured devices, over trusted networks — and data remains stored strictly in secure, UK-managed electronic systems.

7. Virtual Sessions & Workshop Security

Online consultations are delivered using Google Meet over secure, encrypted video calling. To ensure the safe delivery of virtual healthcare, we confirm your full identity, your physical location, and your emergency contact information at or before your first session. We do not record video sessions under any circumstances, and clients are asked not to record sessions, to protect mutual privacy.

Workshops are group settings. You should not share personal health information during a workshop that you would not be comfortable others in the group hearing, as we cannot guarantee the confidentiality of disclosures made by participants in a group environment.

8. Sharing Your Information

Your clinical and personal information is treated with strict confidentiality. It will only be shared under the following conditions:

  • With your express consent: we will only share your details with third parties — such as your GP, other healthcare professionals involved in your care, your employer, insurer, or legal representative — where you have given explicit, written consent.
  • Safeguarding concerns (without consent): we have a professional and statutory duty to share relevant information without your consent where there is a safeguarding concern involving a child or an adult at risk (for example, reporting to local authority adult safeguarding teams or children's services). Wherever safe and appropriate, we will discuss this with you beforehand.
  • Emergencies (without consent): where there is an immediate, substantial risk of harm to you or others, we will share necessary location and identity details with emergency services (999) and/or your GP to protect your vital interests.
  • Legal requirement: where required by a court order, statute, or law enforcement agency, and with the HCPC in the event of a fitness-to-practise investigation.

We also use trusted processors to run the practice and website — our email and records provider (Google Workspace), our website hosting provider, and Google Meet for consultations — all of whom are required to handle your data securely and in accordance with UK data protection law.

9. International Data Transfers

Where your data is transferred outside the UK (for example, by a third-party service provider), we ensure that appropriate safeguards are in place — such as UK adequacy regulations or Standard Contractual Clauses — to protect your data to an equivalent standard.

10. Data Retention

We implement a careful, legally compliant retention schedule:

  • Clinical records: retained for 8 years from the date of last contact, then securely and permanently deleted. This meets our insurer's condition of cover (records kept at least 7 years from the date of treatment — and, for a person lacking capacity, at least 7 years after capacity is regained or after death) and the UK benchmark for adult health records (NHS Records Management Code of Practice).
  • Enquiry data (non-client): deleted within 12 months if no therapeutic relationship is established.
  • Business & financial records: retained for 6 years from the end of the relevant tax year in line with HMRC requirements, stored separately from clinical records where practicable.
  • Technical / analytics data: retained in accordance with our hosting provider's policies (typically up to 26 months).
  • Ongoing legal or regulatory matters: where a claim, complaint, or investigation is ongoing or anticipated, the relevant records are retained until the matter is concluded.

Secure destruction: once the retention period has elapsed, all electronic files on primary systems, emails, and secure backups are permanently and securely deleted. Any temporary paper notes are transferred to electronic records and immediately destroyed.

11. Cookies

This website uses essential, functional browser storage only (to remember your language preference and that you have seen our cookie notice). We do not use advertising or cross-site tracking. Full details are in our separate Cookie Policy.

12. Your Rights Under UK GDPR

You hold the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Right of access (Subject Access Requests): you can request a copy of your personal data and clinical records, in writing or verbally. We respond within one calendar month, using secure, encrypted, or password-protected formats.
  • Right to rectification: to have inaccurate or incomplete information corrected.
  • Right to erasure / restriction: to request deletion or restriction of your data. Please note this does not apply to clinical records we are legally and professionally required to retain for 8 years under our insurance, tax, and clinical obligations.
  • Right to data portability: to receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format.
  • Right to object: to our processing based on legitimate interests.
  • Right to withdraw consent: where processing is based on consent, you may withdraw it at any time, without affecting the lawfulness of prior processing.
